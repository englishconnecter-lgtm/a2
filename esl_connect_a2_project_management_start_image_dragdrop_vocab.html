Reading Comprehension

What is project management?

Read the text and complete the comprehension task.

Project management means planning and organising work so a team can finish a project successfully.

A project usually has a clear goal. For example, a team may need to create a new website, organise an event, prepare a presentation or design a new product.

Before the team starts, they make a simple plan. The plan explains the main tasks, the people responsible for each task and the deadline. A deadline is the date when the work must be finished.

Good project management helps people understand what they need to do. It also helps the team solve problems early. If a task is late, the team can change the plan or give help to the person responsible.

In a good project team, people communicate clearly. They ask questions, give updates and listen to each other. Short meetings can help the team check progress and decide the next steps.

Dialogue

A short project meeting

Read the dialogue and practise it with a partner.

Manager: Good morning. Can we check the welcome lunch project?

Ana: Yes. Our goal is to organise the lunch by Friday.

Manager: Great. What are the main tasks?

Ana: I will choose the place. Marco will order the food. Sara will send the invitation.

Manager: Is everything on time?

Ana: Mostly, yes. But we have one small problem. The café is too expensive.

Manager: Okay. Please check another place and send me an update tomorrow.

Ana: No problem. I’ll send a short update in the morning.

Grammar

Project English grammar drill

Practise the grammar you need for simple project meetings: will for responsibilities and by for deadlines.

1. will + verb = say who agrees to do a task.

I will choose the place. Marco will order the food. We will send an update.

2. by + day/date/time = say the deadline.

We will finish by Friday. I will send the email by 3 p.m.
