Making Appointments

A2 Business English lesson for arranging, changing and confirming appointments.

Start

Lesson overview

In this lesson, students learn simple language for making appointments at work, at a clinic, or on the phone.

Warm-up

When do you usually make appointments? Think about doctors, meetings, interviews, haircuts or school meetings.

Lesson aims

  • Ask if someone is available.
  • Suggest a day and time.
  • Accept or refuse politely.
  • Confirm appointment details.
  • Change an appointment.
Reading

Making an appointment

When you make an appointment, you agree on a day, a time and a place. You can make appointments by phone, by email, online or in person.

It is important to be polite and clear. First, explain why you need the appointment. Then ask about availability. If the first time is not possible, suggest another time.

At the end, confirm the details. You can say: So, that’s Tuesday at 10 o’clock. If you need to change the appointment later, apologise and ask for a new time.

Dialogue

Can we meet on Tuesday?

Anna: Good morning. I’d like to make an appointment with Mr Brown.

Receptionist: Of course. Are you available on Tuesday morning?

Anna: Sorry, Tuesday morning isn’t good for me. Do you have a slot in the afternoon?

Receptionist: Yes, we have 3 p.m.

Anna: That works for me.

Receptionist: Great. So, that’s Tuesday at 3 p.m.

Anna: Thank you. See you then.

Grammar

Would like / Can / Could

I’d like to... is polite: I’d like to make an appointment.

Can / Could are useful for requests: Can we meet on Monday? / Could I change the time?
