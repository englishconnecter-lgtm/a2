Lesson overview

Students practise simple language for job interviews and short professional answers.

Lesson aims

  • Introduce yourself in a job interview.
  • Talk about skills and work experience.
  • Use simple past and can for abilities.
  • Ask polite questions at the end of an interview.

Key idea

A job interview is a formal conversation where an employer asks questions to learn about a candidate.

Reading Comprehension

Before a job interview

Read the text and answer the questions.

Before a job interview, it is important to prepare. First, read the job advert carefully. Think about the skills the company needs and prepare examples from your work, school or volunteer experience.

At the start of the interview, greet the interviewer politely and introduce yourself. Speak clearly and do not answer too quickly. Short, clear answers are better than very long answers.

Interviewers often ask about your experience, skills and availability. You can say what you can do well, what you did in the past and why you want the job.

At the end, you can ask one or two polite questions, for example about the working hours, the team or the next step in the process.

Dialogue

A short interview

Read and practise the dialogue.

Interviewer: Good morning. Please have a seat.

Candidate: Good morning. Thank you.

Interviewer: Can you tell me a little about yourself?

Candidate: Yes. My name is Leo. I am organised and friendly. I worked in a small shop last summer.

Interviewer: What skills do you have?

Candidate: I can speak English and Italian. I can use Excel, and I am good with customers.

Interviewer: When are you available to start?

Candidate: I am available from next Monday.

Interviewer: Do you have any questions?

Candidate: Yes. What are the working hours?

Comprehension

Grammar

can for skills + past simple for experience

Use can to talk about skills and past simple to talk about experience.

Skills: I can use Excel. / I can speak English. / I can work in a team.

Experience: I worked in a shop. / I helped customers. / I studied business at school.

Speaking

Interview role cards

Work in pairs. One student is the interviewer. One student is the candidate.

Card A: Shop Assistant

You are applying for a weekend job in a clothes shop.

Skills: friendly, organised, good with customers.

Card B: Café Assistant

You are applying for a part-time job in a café.

Skills: polite, fast, good in a team.

Card C: Office Assistant

You are applying for a summer office job.

Skills: can use Excel, can write emails, organised.

Card D: Event Helper

You are applying to help at a business event.

Skills: can speak English, friendly, punctual.

Interview checklist

  • Greet the interviewer politely.
  • Say your name and one positive adjective.
  • Talk about one skill using can.
  • Talk about one past experience.
  • Ask one question at the end.

Interview questions

  • Can you tell me a little about yourself?
  • Why do you want this job?
  • What skills do you have?
  • What experience do you have?
  • When are you available to start?
