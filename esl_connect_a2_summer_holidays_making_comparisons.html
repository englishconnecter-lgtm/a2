Summer Holidays

A2 English lesson with grammar focus: making comparisons with comparative adjectives, superlatives and as...as.

A2 Grammar Lesson

Compare summer holiday places

Students compare beaches, cities, mountains and campsites using simple adjectives and travel vocabulary.

LevelA2
Time60 minutes
GrammarComparisons
Start

Lesson overview

Students talk about summer holidays and compare different holiday destinations.

Lesson aims

  • Talk about summer holiday places.
  • Use comparative adjectives: hotter, cheaper, more relaxing.
  • Use superlatives: the hottest, the cheapest, the most exciting.
  • Use as + adjective + as: as beautiful as.

Warm-up

  • Where do you usually go in summer?
  • Do you prefer the beach or the mountains?
  • What is your ideal summer holiday?

Key vocabulary

beach, city, mountains, campsite, hotel, sunny, crowded, relaxing, expensive, cheap, exciting, quiet

Reading Comprehension

Choosing a summer holiday

Read the text and answer the questions.

Anna and Leo want to plan a summer holiday. Anna wants to go to the beach because she loves swimming and sunny weather. She thinks the beach is more relaxing than the city.

Leo prefers the mountains. He says the mountains are quieter than the beach, and the air is fresher. He also thinks a mountain holiday is cheaper than a beach hotel.

Their friend Mia wants to visit a big city. She says a city holiday is more exciting because there are museums, shops and restaurants. However, it can be more expensive and more crowded.

In the end, they choose a campsite near a lake. It is not as crowded as the city, and it is cheaper than a hotel. They can swim, walk and relax.

Grammar

Making comparisons

Learn how to compare holiday places.

1. Comparative adjectives

Use comparatives to compare two things.

AdjectiveComparativeExample
cheapcheaper thanA campsite is cheaper than a hotel.
hothotter thanSpain is hotter than England.
quietquieter thanThe mountains are quieter than the beach.
relaxingmore relaxing thanThe beach is more relaxing than the city.

2. Superlatives

Use superlatives to compare three or more things: the cheapest, the hottest, the quietest, the most exciting.

3. as + adjective + as

Use as + adjective + as when two things are similar: The lake is as beautiful as the beach.

Use not as + adjective + as when they are different: The city is not as quiet as the mountains.

Speaking

Compare holiday places

Work with a partner. Use comparatives, superlatives and as...as.

Speaking prompts

  • Compare a beach holiday and a city holiday.
  • Compare a hotel and a campsite.
  • Compare the mountains and the beach.
  • Say which holiday is the best for you and why.

Useful speaking model

I think a beach holiday is more relaxing than a city holiday. A city holiday is more exciting, but it is also more crowded. For me, the beach is the best place because I like swimming and sunny weather.
