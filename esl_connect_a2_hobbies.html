Hobbies

An A2 English lesson for talking about free-time activities, likes and dislikes, frequency, and making simple plans.

A2 Everyday English

Talk about what you enjoy

Students learn how to describe hobbies, ask follow-up questions, and talk about how often they do activities.

LevelA2
Time60–75 minutes
FocusHobbies & free time
Start

Lesson overview

Students practise talking about hobbies and asking simple questions about free time.

Lesson aims

  • Name common hobbies.
  • Talk about likes and dislikes.
  • Use frequency adverbs: always, usually, often, sometimes, never.
  • Ask and answer questions about hobbies.

Warm-up questions

  • What do you do in your free time?
  • Do you prefer indoor or outdoor hobbies?
  • What hobby would you like to try?

Key idea

A hobby is an activity you do because you enjoy it, not because it is work or school.

Reading Comprehension

Why are hobbies important?

Read the text and answer the questions.

Hobbies are activities we do in our free time. Some people like creative hobbies, such as painting, cooking or playing music. Other people prefer active hobbies, such as swimming, cycling or playing football.

Hobbies are important because they help us relax. They can also help us meet new people and learn new skills. For example, if you join a photography club, you can practise taking photos and make friends.

You do not need a lot of money to have a hobby. Walking, reading, drawing and watching films can be simple and enjoyable hobbies.

When people talk about hobbies, they often ask questions like “What do you do in your free time?” and “How often do you do it?”

Dialogue

Talking about hobbies

Read and practise the dialogue.

Mia: What do you do in your free time?

Leo: I usually play football on Saturdays.

Mia: Nice. How often do you play?

Leo: I play once a week. What about you?

Mia: I like painting and taking photos.

Leo: That sounds fun. Are you in a club?

Mia: Yes, I go to an art club on Wednesdays.

Leo: I’d like to try that one day.

Grammar

Frequency adverbs

Use frequency adverbs to say how often you do something.

Order: always → usually → often → sometimes → never

Position: I usually play football. / She often reads books. / We never go cycling on Monday.

Speaking

Hobby speaking cards

Choose a card. Ask and answer questions with a partner.

Card A: Sporty person

You love football and cycling. You usually exercise at the weekend.

Ask: What sport do you like?

Card B: Creative person

You enjoy painting and photography. You go to an art club once a week.

Ask: Would you like to try painting?

Card C: Relaxed person

You like reading, watching films and cooking at home.

Ask: What do you do to relax?

Card D: Music fan

You play guitar and listen to music every day.

Ask: What kind of music do you like?

Conversation checklist

  • Ask about hobbies.
  • Ask how often your partner does the hobby.
  • Say if you like or do not like the hobby.
  • Show interest with one useful phrase.
  • Ask one follow-up question.
