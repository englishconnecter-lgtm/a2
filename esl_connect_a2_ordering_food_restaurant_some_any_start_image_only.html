Ordering Food in a Restaurant

An A2 English lesson for ordering food, asking polite questions, understanding a menu and speaking with a waiter.

Start

Lesson overview

Students learn useful English for ordering food in a restaurant.

A2 Everyday English

Order food clearly and politely

Students practise restaurant vocabulary, polite requests, menu language and short role plays for ordering food.

LevelA2
Time60–75 minutes
FocusRestaurant English

Lesson aims

  • Understand basic restaurant vocabulary.
  • Ask for a table and order food politely.
  • Use I’d like, Can I have and Could I have.
  • Practise a restaurant role play.

Warm-up questions

  • How often do you eat in restaurants?
  • What food do you usually order?
  • Do you prefer starters, main courses or desserts?

Dialogue

At the restaurant

Read and practise the dialogue.

Waiter: Good evening. Do you have a reservation?

Customer: Yes, a table for two under the name Rossi.

Waiter: Great. Please follow me. Here are the menus.

Customer: Thank you. What do you recommend?

Waiter: The grilled chicken is very popular.

Customer: Okay. I’d like the tomato soup as a starter and the grilled chicken as a main course.

Waiter: Would you like anything to drink?

Customer: Could I have still water, please?

Waiter: Of course.

Comprehension

Grammar

Polite requests with some and any

Use polite phrases when ordering food. Practise some and any in restaurant questions and answers.

Polite requests

I’d like + noun: I’d like the pasta.

Can I have + noun?: Can I have the bill, please?

Could I have + noun?: Could I have some water, please?

Some and any

UseExample
some in positive sentencesThere is some cheese in the salad.
some in polite offers and requestsWould you like some bread? / Could I have some water?
any in questionsDo you have any vegetarian dishes?
any in negative sentencesThere isn’t any fish today.

Speaking

Restaurant role play

Work in pairs. One student is the waiter. One student is the customer.

Customer Card A

You want a table for two. Order tomato soup, chicken with rice and still water. Ask if the chicken has garlic.

Customer Card B

You want a table for one. Order salad, pasta and sparkling water. Ask for the bill at the end.

Waiter Card

Welcome the customer. Give the menu. Recommend one dish. Ask about drinks. Bring the bill.

Challenge Card

The restaurant is busy. Politely say one dish is not available and suggest another dish.

Speaking support

Good evening. Do you have a reservation? / Can I see the menu, please? / I’d like... / Could I have...? / What do you recommend? / Can we have the bill, please?
