Going to the Doctor

An A2 English lesson for describing symptoms, making an appointment, understanding advice and speaking politely at the doctor’s office.

A2 Everyday English

Explain a simple health problem

Students learn useful language for doctor appointments, symptoms, advice and short patient–doctor conversations.

LevelA2
Time60–75 minutes
FocusHealth & appointments
Start

Lesson overview

Students learn simple language for visiting a doctor and explaining common symptoms.

Lesson aims

  • Name common symptoms.
  • Make a simple doctor’s appointment.
  • Use have got / has got for symptoms.
  • Understand and give simple health advice.
  • Role-play a short visit to the doctor.

Warm-up

  • When do people go to the doctor?
  • What symptoms can you describe in English?
  • Do you prefer calling or booking online?

Useful start

I don’t feel well.
I’ve got a headache.
I’d like to make an appointment.

Dialogue

At the doctor’s office

Read the dialogue and answer the questions.

Receptionist: Good morning. How can I help you?

Patient: Good morning. I’d like to see a doctor, please.

Receptionist: What seems to be the problem?

Patient: I’ve got a sore throat and a cough.

Receptionist: Do you have a fever?

Patient: Yes, I think so.

Receptionist: The doctor can see you at 11:30.

Patient: Thank you. That’s fine.

Grammar

have got / has got for symptoms

Use have got to describe health problems.

I / You / We / They have got... a cough.

He / She has got... a fever.

Question: Have you got a headache? / Has she got a sore throat?

Functional Language

Useful phrases at the doctor

Practise polite A2 phrases.

SituationPhrase
Make an appointmentI’d like to make an appointment.
Explain the problemI don’t feel well.
Describe symptomsI’ve got a cough and a fever.
Ask for adviceWhat should I do?
Understand adviceYou should rest and drink water.
Speaking

Doctor role-play cards

Work in pairs. Student A is the patient. Student B is the doctor or receptionist.

Card 1: Headache

You have got a headache. Ask for an appointment. Ask what you should do.

Card 2: Sore throat

You have got a sore throat and a cough. Ask for advice.

Card 3: Stomach ache

You have got a stomach ache. Say when it started.

Card 4: Fever

You have got a fever. Ask if you need medicine.

Role-play support

Patient: Hello. I’d like to see a doctor. I’ve got... What should I do?

Doctor: You should rest, drink water and take this medicine. Come back if you feel worse.

Review

Final check

Review the key language.

Exit task

Give a 30-second patient role-play. Say your symptom, ask for advice and thank the doctor.
