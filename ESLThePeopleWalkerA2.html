Lesson Overview

Students watch a short video about Chuck McCarthy, a man in Hollywood who walks with people as a job. The lesson practises A2 language for jobs, exercise, companionship and simple present / present perfect.

Before you watch

  1. Do you like walking? Why or why not?
  2. Would you pay someone to walk with you?
  3. Is walking good for your body and your mind?

A2 Grammar: Present Simple vs Present Continuous

Use the present simple for routines, facts and things that are generally true. Use the present continuous for actions happening now or temporary situations.

TenseUseExample from the topic
Present simplefacts and routinesI live in Hollywood. / I meet up with people.
Present simplegeneral truthsWalking is amazing exercise.
Present continuousactions happening nowI am walking with a customer now.
Present continuoustemporary situationsToday, I am meeting a new client.

Student B: Customer
