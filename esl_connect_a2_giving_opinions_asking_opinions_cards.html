Reading

Why opinions are important

Read the short text and answer the questions.

People have different opinions. At school, at work and with friends, we often talk about ideas. Sometimes we agree. Sometimes we disagree.

It is important to be polite when we give an opinion. We can say, I think... or In my opinion.... These phrases show that we are sharing our idea, not a fact.

When we agree, we can say, I agree or That’s true. When we disagree, we should be careful. We can say, I’m not sure I agree or I see your point, but.... This is polite and respectful.

A good discussion is not a fight. People listen, give reasons and ask questions. Short reasons with because help other people understand our opinion.

Dialogue

Choosing a class project

Read and practise the dialogue.

Mia: I think we should make a video for the class project.

Leo: I agree. A video is interesting.

Sara: I’m not sure I agree. A video takes a lot of time.

Mia: I see your point, but we can make a short video.

Leo: That’s true. In my opinion, a short video is a good idea.

Sara: Okay, I agree. Let’s make a short video and use simple language.

Grammar Drill

Project English grammar drill: opinion + reason

Use simple structures to give opinions and reasons.

Structure 1: I think + sentence.
I think this plan is good.

Structure 2: opinion + because + reason.
I agree because it is simple.

Structure 3: I’m not sure I agree because...
I’m not sure I agree because it is expensive.
