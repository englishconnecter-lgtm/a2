Buying Clothes

An A2 English lesson for shopping for clothes, asking about sizes, colours, prices and trying things on.

A2 Everyday English

Shop for clothes confidently

Students learn useful phrases for clothes shopping, asking for help and saying what fits or does not fit.

LevelA2
Time60–75 minutes
FocusShopping & clothes
Start

Lesson overview

Students practise buying clothes in a shop using simple A2 language.

Lesson aims

  • Name common clothes and accessories.
  • Ask about size, colour and price.
  • Use this/that/these/those for clothes.
  • Practise a shop assistant and customer dialogue.

Warm-up questions

  • Do you like shopping for clothes?
  • What clothes do you buy most often?
  • Do you prefer shops or online shopping?

Key idea

In a clothes shop, you often ask about size, colour, price and the fitting room.

Reading Comprehension

At a clothes shop

Read the text and answer the questions.

When you buy clothes in a shop, you can ask a shop assistant for help. You can ask for a different size, another colour or the price.

If you want to try something on, you can ask: “Can I try this on?” The shop assistant can show you the fitting room.

After you try the clothes on, you can say if they fit well. For example, “It fits well,” “It’s too small,” or “It’s too big.”

At the end, you can pay by card or in cash. If you do not want to buy the item, you can say, “I’ll leave it, thank you.”

Dialogue

Buying a jacket

Read and practise the dialogue.

Assistant: Hello. Can I help you?

Customer: Yes, please. I’m looking for a jacket.

Assistant: What size are you?

Customer: I’m a medium. Do you have this jacket in blue?

Assistant: Yes, here you are.

Customer: Thanks. Can I try it on?

Assistant: Of course. The fitting rooms are over there.

Customer: It fits well. How much is it?

Assistant: It’s £45.

Customer: Great. I’ll take it.

Comprehension

Grammar

this, that, these, those

Use these words to talk about clothes near or far from you.

Singular near: this jacket

Singular far: that jacket

Plural near: these trousers

Plural far: those shoes

Speaking

Shopping role cards

Work in pairs. One student is the customer. One student is the shop assistant.

Card A: Customer

You want to buy a blue jacket. Ask for a medium. Try it on and buy it if it fits.

Useful phrase: Do you have this in a medium?

Card B: Shop Assistant

You work in a clothes shop. Help the customer with size, colour and price.

Useful phrase: The fitting rooms are over there.

Card C: Customer

You want to buy black trousers. They are too long. Ask for a smaller size.

Useful phrase: Do you have a smaller size?

Card D: Shop Assistant

The trousers are £30. There is a 10% discount today.

Useful phrase: They are on sale today.

Conversation checklist

  • Ask for help.
  • Ask about size or colour.
  • Ask to try something on.
  • Say if it fits.
  • Buy it or leave it politely.
