Key idea

Networking means meeting people, making professional connections and starting useful conversations.

Reading Comprehension

What is networking?

Read the text and answer the questions.

Networking is when you meet people and make professional connections. You can network at conferences, meetings, training days, company events or online.

A good networking conversation is usually short and polite. First, you introduce yourself. Then, you ask simple questions. You can ask about a person’s job, company or reason for attending the event.

It is important to listen carefully. Good networking is not only talking about yourself. It is also showing interest in the other person.

At the end of the conversation, you can exchange contact details or say that it was nice to meet them. Later, you can send a short follow-up message.

Dialogue

At a networking event

Read and practise the dialogue.

Anna: Hi, I’m Anna. Nice to meet you.

Ben: Nice to meet you too. I’m Ben.

Anna: What do you do?

Ben: I work in marketing. How about you?

Anna: I’m a project assistant. I help organise events.

Ben: That sounds interesting. Is this your first time at this event?

Anna: Yes, it is. What about you?

Ben: I came last year too. It was very useful.

Anna: Great. Can I have your email?

Ben: Of course. Let’s stay in touch.

Grammar

Present simple questions

Use present simple questions to ask about work and routines.

Question: What do you do?

Answer: I work in marketing. / I’m a project assistant.

Question: Do you often come to events like this?

Answer: Yes, I do. / No, I don’t.

Speaking

Networking role cards

Choose a card. Meet your partner and have a short networking conversation.

Card A: Marketing Assistant

You work for a small fashion company. You want to meet people who organise events.

Useful question: What kind of events do you organise?

Card B: Project Assistant

You help teams plan projects. You want to find new business contacts.

Useful question: What projects are you working on now?

Card C: Sales Trainee

You are new in your company. You want to practise introducing yourself.

Useful question: Is this your first time at this event?

Card D: Event Coordinator

You organise business events. You want to exchange contact details.

Useful question: Can I have your email?

Conversation checklist

  • Introduce yourself.
  • Ask what the other person does.
  • Ask one follow-up question.
  • Show interest.
  • End politely.
