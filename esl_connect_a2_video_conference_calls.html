Lesson overview

Students practise joining a video call, checking sound/video, giving short updates and asking for help.

Lesson aims

  • Use basic video-call vocabulary.
  • Say when there is a technical problem.
  • Ask people to repeat or speak more slowly.
  • Give a short online meeting update.

Warm-up

  • Do you use video calls for school or work?
  • What problems can happen in online meetings?
  • Do you prefer video calls or face-to-face meetings?
Reading

A good video conference call

Read the short text and answer the questions.

A video conference call is an online meeting. People can speak, listen, see each other and share information on a screen.

Before a call, it is important to check your camera, microphone and internet connection. You should join the meeting a few minutes early.

During the call, speak clearly and do not speak too fast. If you have a problem, explain it politely. For example, you can say, “Sorry, my microphone isn’t working” or “Could you repeat that, please?”

At the end of the meeting, people often agree on the next steps. They may decide who will do each task and when the task must be finished.

Dialogue

Joining a video call

Practise the dialogue with a partner.

Emma: Good morning. Can everyone hear me?

Luca: Yes, I can hear you.

Sofia: Sorry, I can’t see your screen.

Emma: No problem. I’ll share it again.

Luca: Could you repeat the first point, please?

Emma: Of course. The first point is the project deadline.

Sofia: Thanks. My connection is a little slow today.

Emma: That’s okay. Let’s speak slowly and check the next steps.

Grammar

can / can’t for online meetings

Use can and can’t to talk about what is possible in a video call.

Positive: I can hear you. / We can see your screen.

Negative: I can’t hear you. / She can’t join the call.

Question: Can you hear me? / Can you repeat that?

Speaking

Video-call role plays

Work with a partner. Use the phrases from the lesson.

Card 1: Bad sound

You are in a meeting. You cannot hear your partner. Ask them to repeat and speak more slowly.

Card 2: Screen sharing

You are presenting. Your partner cannot see your screen. Share it again and check if it works.

Card 3: Late joiner

You join the call two minutes late. Apologise and ask what the team is discussing.

Card 4: Next steps

You are the meeting leader. End the call and agree on two next steps.

Speaking support

Sorry, I’m late. / Can you hear me? / I can’t see your screen. / Could you repeat that, please? / Let’s check the next steps.

Review

Final review

Exit task
